Facebook Dating

Project Overview
Facebook has been working on a new dating app that focuses on introducing people to others in their local community.
Need Statement
How can we increase the total maximum number of matches people can earn on Facebook Dating?

Tools used include : Sketch, Figma, Origmai, Proton, and Python for Machine learning.
"How can we get user's more matches?"

To deal with that need statement, I looked at upcoming dating apps with fast download growth like Blinder, Tinder, and OkCupid to pool data and I finally found my own pain points based on common reviews found on the Google Playstore.
‍
 My personal approach to this problem is to account for the fact that different people have different love languages and they aren’t all based on physical looks.  So I am building a blind dating  add on as an approach to fixing this problem While some people may rely soley on physical looks, some people must be fine with soley personal connections.

Research Goals!


Their were 4 main things I wanted to get from my research. My research goals helped me chose what kind of interview questions I would ask the 30 people in interviewed.

But first, let’s start with value. I made sure to check if their was a market for blind dating apps so I started with the app store. The 3 blind dating apps I chose to analyze included Blindr, Bumble, and Happn.  

‍
Data Engineering


Based on the number of installs and high ratings, you would assume that plenty of people use blind dating apps all the time.

However, after conducting research with 30 Facebook interns, it was very clear that telling people to go on a “blind date” vs a typical date wasn’t very successful.

‍8 out of 10 people said they wouldn’t consider a feature like that.

Simply put, they couldn’t validate the safety of such a feature and they would typically never use dating apps as a whole.

However, 9 out of 10 people said they liked they would wanna meet a new person at an event they had in common.

So I decided to pivot my blind dating app to be more of an event based dating app; one where local Facebook events had the option of listing potential matches with people who would like to meet up at an event together.

My Design Iterations

I started off working with the current Facebook Dating Style by building it in Origami. I mainly learned how this was an efficnet system for the current dating app, but with my blind dating idea, it actually was found extremely "creepy" and "gave me wanna-call-the-cops vibes".

So in the next iteration, I built a system where you could see people's faces, but you had to find them using Facebook Events! And there you can opt into a group chat to blind date.




When making the blind dating match up system, I realized that I couldn't pair people up this late in the game since it was an extra feature that users would need to go though. So I had to scrap this flow approach and I ended up building a new design that was pitched to the facebook dating team! I unfortunately cannot show that design, but I got permission to show this one!
