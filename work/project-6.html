Thoyen

Project Overview
Thoyen is a student based app made by students for students to help each other find work and help with basic services.
 Thoyen’s engagement among Texas A&M students is relatively low and had non-existent retention rate.
Need Statement
How can we make this product be used by more students?

Tools used include Invision, Figma, Sketch, UX research, Python ( Machine learning) and Origami.

What is Thoyen? 

Thoyen is a mobile app for college students that is based in Los Angeles with the goal of creating job opportunities for students and by students. It's function could be similar to Craigslist, except this app's function relies on it's quick system that matches students who are looking for work with students that need a job done. Like if a student needs a ride somewhere, they can call for a ride on the app and all possible drivers will be notified instantly. This app also features other jobs students can do like car washing, house cleaning, and specialist work like app design.

Sounds great! Why don't I know about this yet? 

Great question! Right now, it is only launched in 2 cities ( soon 4! ). One in College Station for Texas A&M Students and another in Los Angeles for USC (University of Southern California ) students. When they moved to College Station though, the concept of this app wasn't taken too well. Mainly because the nature of students was different then USC students.

The problems that USC students and TAMU ( Texas A&M ) students are extremely different. For instance, in USC, Uber and Lyft are fairly exepensive and it may cost $18 - $22 to travel 2 miles on a good day. So traveling long distances from LA to San Deigo can be extemely expensive using Lyft or Uber. So this app can actually compete to make inexpensive ride prices with people who are on their way to the same location.

However, in College Station, it is common for Ubers and Lyfts to cost $4-$8 for a 2 mile drive. And that is just if you want to pay; we also have programs like Zimride or Zipride to travel hundreds of miles with other students for $10 or less! And we have local programs like Carpool that provide free rides to anyone traveling in the Bryan/College Station area.

‍

So as you can guess, many people in College Station felt the app's features weren't useful because we have free solutions for these problems like donation based car washes and $1 house keeping service. And not just at A&M, but many Texas Universities have a budget to make transportation more affordable and improve student quality of life. So I was tasked with making options for Thoyen and 2 really stood out for me : 1 ) move to a private school in Texas that doesn't have a budget for tavel and student living 2) get new features that will be used by Texans.

What did I do? 

Since I go to Texas A&M and I believed in the apps mission, I took the latter and tried to find a new feature for this app that could be used by students. I got approval from the CEO and began interviewing students to find my brand new features while also being tasked with meeting certain business requirements I had to meet during my internship as a whole.

Reflections

This project was fun to work on ! And ultimately, my feature was shipped and it will be launched completely soon! However, as a part time developer, I also quickly coded my interaction style into the app's main code bay. Take aways

While this project was an effective success, I didn't know just how much a person's location can effect the kinds of problems that people can face. I will be sure to consider peoples locations in my designs in the feature as well as how that can work with a businesses goals in the future.
