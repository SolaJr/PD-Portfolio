Balloon Grid Tool

Role - Mechanical Product Designer

Client - Texas A&M Architecture Department

Time - August 2017 to May 2018

Tools - Solidworks, metal working, wood working, and UX research

Brief - Modern architecture has been moving away from concrete buildings due to high expences associated with complex designs. However, since most of the cost for a building comes from the labors developing it, we decided to create a press that could simplify the process of making concrete roofs. So we developed a multipoint actuator to simplify the process of making presses for the concrete. That way, instead of wasting thousands of dollars on wood and materials to build frames, our Balloon Grid press can form them with a single person. If you would like to speak about this project more, please reach out to me since I can't say too much publicly.

Need Statement - Develop novel method of construction for complex-curved forms for building with an adjustable multi-point incremental forming mold maker.

Contribuitions include: Pitched methods to make interesting shapes possible at low cost. Researched concrete mixing methods and material science principles to select optimal concrete for testingCommunicated with a variety of students from other academic Recorded and edited a short film introducing the college of engineering to the prototype.

My Success matrix

What I learned

Visual Design

Planning my hardware / 3D projects in 2D views and sketches is crucial to creating a good design. Postponing it until the functionatlity is done can be really disappointing since I couldn't change the design much after it was built.

Interaction Design

This product was designed to be operated by a labview program where the user inputs the z axis displacement for each of the 6 cubes. This may be simple to me but is difficult for many to understand. I should have made a UI interface to guide them though the process or have preset modes.

Intentional Design

This was my first project working with people on the techincal aspect of a project. So I needed to make tasks and manage risk by chating with my team one on one casually so I could learn where they excel and divide their work accordingly.

Product Thinking

Since I am the one who invented this solution, I managed the product thinking to a high extent. Ultimately I build the MVP to be sturdy enough to withstand constant examinations by the Civil Engineering departnemtns research team.

Productivity

I managed the entire success of the product and developed it in a few short monthes as well as presented it to countless research competitions.

Self Awareness

This project showed me the importance of making specific design requirements at the beginning of the problem solving phase. Otherwise, I may end up focusing on creating my own design requirements as I go own instead
