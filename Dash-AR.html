Dash - AR

Role - Product Designer / Game Developer , UX Designer

Client - Startup Aggieland ( Start up )

Time - November 2018 to Febuary 2019

Tools - C#, Unity3D, Keynote, and Sarcasm

Brief - How can we make college students enjoy fitness with incentive?

Need Statement - By using Virtual Reality or Augmented Reality, create a product that can encourage continued healthy living. The end result was an Augmented Reality program that would allow a user to make the practice of running or walking more enjoyable by transforming it into a game with features allowing you to race your friends, run from zombies, or simply race past versions of yourself.

My solution was to make a partnership with Netflix for a virtual game I call Dash AR. The idea behind Dash AR is that if you go for a run any time the app is on, you can race your time against someone else in the world ( or rather on campus) If your time is slower then your opponent, then you need to watch a 30 second ad on your phone. Mean while the winner gets a few Dash Tokens that can be used to redeem a month of free Netflix! The money for the Netflix comes from a percentage of of the ad revenue genrated by the loser who watched the ad. This project was given to someone else so their are limits as to what I can say about it ( an NDA ) so if you wanna learn more, check out these two demo videos I made and email me at OlusolaBabatunde.sb@gmail.com .

What I learned

Visual Design

Designing in a 3D space takes a lot of planning. I need to make 3D storyboards because of how fragile the Unity3D environment is.

Interaction Design

Plan hands-free transitions. This product was developed using a speech recognition app system. However, without a neuronet, accents can often be missed. Hand's free solutions may be the best for interaction design in VR / AR since hand gear is pretty expensive inheriently.

Intentional Design

I designed Dash AR so that it could be used in a light weight mobile system. I prevented the AR from occupying more then 25% of a screen's visibility so that people would be safe walking around in busy areas.

Product Thinking

In order to make this product be launched to an interested population that can readily provide it's own metavision AR and VR equipment for users, I pitched this product to Texas A&M University. I focused on building the app around A&M's color schemes and students habits by populating and testing the college station area's map.

Productivity

I developed all the code and 3D modeling and world designing for this app as well as the user interface.

Self Awareness

I realize that I have a lot to learn about programming, game building, and world designing as well as unity3D . But I think I will start focusing on different ways to get people to interact with something.
